THREE POINTS: 1. SCALE: 9,000 training buildings, 900 test buildings never seen in training (strict building-level split). Mean NRMSE 0.98%, median 0.89% — comfortably under 1% on a diverse portfolio. 2. HEIGHT UNIFORMITY: The height-normalized interpolation scheme maps any 10/15/20 storey building onto 20 canonical height levels. Error is nearly identical across the three height classes (1.05/1.01/0.88%) — the interpolation removes the floor-count dependency. 3. DURATION GENERALIZATION (separate test — different from the 9k NRMSE above): The network was trained only on 60-second wind records. When tested on 600-second signals (10× longer, never seen in training), RMSE = 0.040 — only a moderate increase despite a tenfold extension in duration. The model captures structural dynamics rather than memorising a fixed temporal template. (RMSE 0.040 is a raw displacement error in the same units as the response; it is NOT the same metric as the NRMSE % headline — intentionally kept off the slide to avoid confusion.)
