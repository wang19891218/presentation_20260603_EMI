Single-building diagnostic (RMSE on building 0) — separate from the 160-case benchmark NRMSE (0.017).

This is an ablation study on the weight-generator (WGM) architecture. All numbers come from a single-building diagnostic on building 0 — do NOT conflate RMSE 0.012 with the population NRMSE 0.017 headline. MLP weight generator: the standard 500-epoch run consistently collapses. Loss plateaus at approximately 0.0136; the model's output std-ratio falls below 5% of the target signal's std — it is effectively predicting the mean at every timestep (flat-output failure). Only a 2000-epoch progressive training schedule breaks the plateau, reaching a best RMSE of 0.070. That is still approximately 5.8× worse than the Transformer, and requires 4× the training compute. Transformer weight generator: RMSE 0.012, achieved routinely in a standard 500-epoch run. No collapse observed. The structural reason: each of the seven building features (H, W, D, ζ, ρ, N_s, f₁) is embedded as its own token, and the two-layer self-attention encoder models cross-feature interactions explicitly before generating the response-network weights. The MLP has no such structure — it cannot discover those interactions reliably within the available parameter budget and training budget. Key message: the choice of Transformer as the weight generator is not cosmetic — it is the critical design decision that prevents the model from collapsing.
