Looking ahead

Future directions.

  • More complex loads — the immediate next step. Improve impulse coverage; extend to blast, multi-hazard, and arbitrary broadband excitations.
  • Physics-informed / knowledge-enhanced training — embed governing equations to improve extrapolation and cut data demand for larger, more diverse inventories.
  • Richer features + 3D models — image and BIM-driven building representations; extend to 3D FEM with torsional and complex nonlinear behavior.
Keep this slide forward-looking and light. The model is a strong working foundation; these are natural, well-motivated next steps. Most immediate: extending to more complex loads. Impulse at 54.2% NRMSE is the clearest gap to close first. The training amplitude envelope [0.48, 1.16] MN is the most tractable near-term fix — regenerating the impulse dataset under U(0.5, 5.0) MN would close the OOD gap at the cost of a new generation run. Phase drift under sustained near-resonance sine (~39 cycles over 60 s) is a known limitation of autoregressive LSTM rollout; a phase-aware loss term is the clean separable extension. Physics-informed / knowledge-enhanced training (KEDL-style, Wang 2020) would embed governing equations into the training objective, improving extrapolation and reducing data demand. This is future work — the current model is a pure time-domain Huber-loss objective. Richer features (images, BIM) and 3D structural models are longer-horizon directions that would substantially broaden the applicability of the framework.
